Expression-Gated Consciousness

The Gate Assessment

Discover how your expression changes under pressure. Two short writing tasks. Five minutes. A window into how your mind gates its own depth.

Based on the EGC framework by Bryan Leonard & Brandyn Leonard

How it works

You'll complete two short writing prompts about the same topic. Each takes 2-3 minutes. We'll measure how your expression shifts between them.

  • Task 1 is a relaxed free-write with no pressure
  • Task 2 uses the same prompt, but framed differently
  • Your results reveal your expression type under the EGC model

What we measure

We look at three signals across your two responses: volume (how much you write), lexical diversity (range of vocabulary), and entropy (information density). How these shift reveals whether pressure opens or closes your expressive gate.

Task 1 of 2

Free Writing

Take your time. There are no right or wrong answers. Just write naturally.

Think of a place that has been meaningful to you. It could be somewhere you grew up, a place you visited, or anywhere that holds significance. Describe this place and why it matters to you. Write freely and naturally — there is no word limit and no evaluation.
